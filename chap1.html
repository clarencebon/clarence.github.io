Functional Silos

Definition: Workplace functional silos are divisions of teams depending on based on their role or the division of the workforce by function. The term alludes to vertical feeding silos used for grain storage. Each team is separated from the others from the top. Functional silos in businesses result in two main problems: the separation of territorialization of the workplace and the allocation of resources.


Separation of Resource

Definition: This utilizes more real resources, which means people do not move between the vertical containers, nor do their knowledge or original ideas. Because employees are restricted to the group in which they are employed, managers cannot utilize the finest talent, resources, or knowledge available. Because they are “outside” of their silo, they require a project.


Territorialization Of The Workplace

Definition: It describes how teams are set up in silos. Rapidly adopt a “us vs. them” mentality. They view tasks or endeavors as “theirs” as opposed to those that can be shared by the entire organization. It produces polarized groupings. This can sometimes be as easy as a snide comment about HR, but in the long run, it can also create serious rifts, foster a toxic work atmosphere, and ultimately hurt the business as a whole.
